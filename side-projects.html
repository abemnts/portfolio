I have an AI for that

As my MBA wrapped up, I realized there was more to my skill set than a resume could capture. I wanted one place to show projects, case studies, and the smaller wins that do not fit neatly on a one-page document.

With a post-MBA budget, hiring a developer was not the move. So I used AI tools to move quickly, test structure, revise copy, and publish a functional portfolio that could gather feedback.

The point was not to pretend I am a full-time web developer. The point was to show product instincts: define the user, shape the flow, ship a version, learn from feedback, and keep improving.

Takeaways

  • Building the site made the portfolio itself a product: scoped, prioritized, shipped, and improved through feedback.
  • Structured feedback beat passive browsing. A good discussion guide surfaced better insights than simply sending people a link.
  • AI tools are most powerful when paired with product judgment, not used as a substitute for it.
