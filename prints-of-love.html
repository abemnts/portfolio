Case study

Prints of Love Growth Investment Exploration

Evaluating a customer monetization opportunity through research, journey mapping, prototyping, and ROI analysis for an e-commerce printing business.

RoleProduct Manager on a four-person team
ProductE-commerce wedding and event printing platform
UsersNewlyweds-to-be and event planners
GoalMonetize customers at or after transaction

Problem

Prints of Love wanted to better monetize customers during or after a transaction. The opportunity was not simply to add more products; the team first needed to understand which customers had a strong unmet need and where that need appeared in their planning journey.

The clearest problem centered on newlyweds-to-be who were reluctant to request RSVPs and gather mailing addresses because they worried about annoying friends and family. That hesitation created disengagement from guest management workflows.

My Role

I functioned as a Product Manager, helping lead research into the next growth opportunity, assess strategic fit, and evaluate ROI. I collaborated with teammates working across product management, UX research, and financial planning analysis.

Users and Stakeholders

The primary user persona was a newlywed-to-be in the post-engagement planning phase. These customers had often chosen a wedding style and were moving into final logistics, including RSVPs, addresses, and guest communication.

The business stakeholder was Prints of Love, an e-commerce platform that benefits from organic acquisition through designer referrals and needed growth opportunities aligned with its low outbound marketing model.

Discovery

We conducted weekly user interviews across two iterative rounds of customer research and two rounds of proposed product testing, engaging more than 60 users in total. The team used screeners, personal networks, and recurring synthesis sessions to narrow broad wedding-planning friction into one sharper problem.

Our research showed that guest communication carried emotional friction. Customers wanted logistical clarity without feeling like they were nagging people they cared about.

Concept direction for an address collection tool that reduces manual follow-up and guest communication anxiety.

Decision and Tradeoffs

The team considered broader upsell opportunities like event keepsakes, signage, and related printed goods. We focused on address collection because it was close to the wedding invitation workflow, connected directly to customer pain, and could create a bridge to additional wedding planning features.

Solution

We proposed an address collection digital tool integrated with the Prints of Love website. The tool would automate follow-ups and address management, helping couples gather information without repeatedly pestering friends and family.

Embedded prototype for exploring the address collection solution.

Impact

The proposed solution created a customer-centered growth path: solve an emotionally loaded planning problem, increase guest-management engagement, and create better timing for related product upsells.

ROI model used to assess projected financial impact.

Metrics

  • Increased adoption of guest management tools.
  • Reduced bounce rate from guest management workflows.
  • Improved completion rate for RSVP and address collection flows.
  • Higher conversion to related wedding planning features.
  • Improved user confidence measured through surveys and qualitative feedback.

Reflection

This project reinforced that growth work starts with a precise customer problem. The strongest monetization path was not the most obvious product add-on; it was the workflow that reduced friction at a high-stress moment in the customer journey.
